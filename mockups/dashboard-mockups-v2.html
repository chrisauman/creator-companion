A quiet morning at the studio

487 words · Calm
studio morning pages light

Got there before sunrise. The light through the east window was the kind of gold you only catch maybe ten times a year — that strange honey color that makes the wood floor look like it's lit from underneath. I sat with coffee and didn't open the laptop for almost an hour, which is unusual for me.

The studio felt different at that hour. Less mine, somehow. Like I was visiting a place that belonged to whoever was here yesterday and would be here tomorrow. I started writing in the notebook instead of typing — slower, but my hand stayed ahead of my thinking, which never happens with the laptop.

I'm trying to remember how it felt the first time I set up a workspace in a corner of my bedroom and called it a studio. There was something about that smaller version that I want back.

What's new in v2

  • Three modes for the right column — Today (default), Reading (entry), Composing (new entry). Switch using the buttons above the mockup.
  • Stats stay always visible in a slim horizontal strip at the top — big serif numbers, four columns, never goes away regardless of what mode you're in.
  • List always visible on left — click any entry to swap the right column. No back button needed on desktop. Active entry highlighted with cyan left bar.
  • "Today" pill at top-left of the right column — click it to return from Reading or Composing back to Today's prompts.

Today panel — four ways to start writing

  • Your Daily Spark (hero) — the curated quote with a primary "Start an entry from this →" button, plus heart and share icons.
  • A small prompt — rotating brief prompts from a library. Click the shuffle icon to cycle ("What excited you?", "What got in the way?", "What did you almost write but didn't?", etc.). Click "Start writing →" to compose with that prompt.
  • Begin with how you feel — tap any of the 8 line-icon moods. Compose opens with that mood pre-selected.
  • Continue a thread — smart suggestion based on recent tags ("You wrote about the studio twice this week. Pick that thread back up?"). Paid feature.
  • Just begin — dashed-border card for users who want a blank page, no prompt.

Entry rows — simplified

  • Calendar date + time, single-line title (or first ~80 chars of body if no title — see entry on May 1), small mood line at the bottom with line-icon and word. No photo thumb, no count, no tags. Clean.

Reading view

  • Generous serif title, drop cap on first paragraph, hero image, tags shown here (warm beige), Edit button top-right turns this into the editor inline. Bottom: prev/next entry navigation so users can read sequentially without going back to the list.

Compose view

  • Inline editor in the same right column. Prompt banner shown at top when composing was launched from a prompt. Autosave indicator. Compact tool row for mood/photo/tag.

Sidebar additions

  • Collapse chevron at the top (toggles to icon-only mode). Bottom: clickable user card with full name + paid status, plus a separate gear icon (settings/account) and a logout icon.
