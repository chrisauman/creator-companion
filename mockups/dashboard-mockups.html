What's different about Mockup 1:
  • Stats strip across the top — always visible on desktop, four numbers in a quiet horizontal row with mini streak dots, no chunky cards.
  • Personal greeting — "Good morning, Chris" + date sets a calm tone instead of jumping straight to a button.
  • Compose moves to a discreet pill — "+ New Entry" in the top-right, dark by default, turns cyan on hover. No more giant full-width button.
  • Entry rows redesigned — large serif title leads, a 2-line content peek follows (far more evocative than just metadata), then a soft meta line. Calendar widget shrinks to a quiet date column.
  • Daily Spark elevated — moves to a sticky right rail in a dark feature card with a glowing cyan dot, three quick actions (favorite, share, use-in-entry).
  • Mood check-in is new — eight clean line-icon moods in a 4×2 grid below Spark. One-tap to log how you're feeling. Simple, monochrome, modern.
  • Subtle month dividers — small caps + hairline, not heavy headers.

Mockup 2 — Dual Pane (reading mode)

Email/Notes-app pattern. Middle column is a scannable entry list with stats up top; right column is a generous reading view of the selected entry. Great for desktop because users can browse and read without losing context. The compose CTA stays in the top-right of the preview pane.

A quiet morning at the studio

3 photos 487 words Calm studio morning pages

Got there before sunrise. The light through the east window was the kind of gold you only catch maybe ten times a year — that strange honey color that makes the wood floor look like it's lit from underneath. I sat with coffee and didn't open the laptop for almost an hour, which is unusual for me.

The studio felt different at that hour. Less mine, somehow. Like I was visiting a place that belonged to whoever was here yesterday and would be here tomorrow. I started writing in the notebook instead of typing — slower, but my hand stayed ahead of my thinking, which never happens with the laptop.

What's different about Mockup 2:
  • Two-pane reading layout — list on the left, full entry preview on the right. Like Mail, Notes, Linear. Lets you browse and re-read without leaving the page.
  • Compact stats — three numbers in a small floating card at the top of the list, doesn't take much vertical room.
  • Selected-entry state — black row with cyan date stands out clearly without being heavy.
  • Beautiful reading view — large serif title, drop cap on the first paragraph, hero image, generous line-height. Makes journals feel like the published essays they kind of are.
  • Where Spark + mood go in this layout — could live as a slim top strip above the preview, or in a collapsible third column. (Same content as Mockup 1, just relocated.)

Modern mood icons — a closer look

One-color, line-style icons replacing emoji. Default state is hairline outline; selected state fills with the dark ink. Hover shows cyan. Same family/visual weight throughout — clean, modern, not cartoony.

Color philosophy:
  • Sidebar stays dark (you love it — untouched).
  • Content area is warm off-white (#faf8f4) so uploaded photos and writing dominate visually.
  • Black is used as accent only — primary CTA, Spark feature card, selected entry. Never overwhelming.
  • Cyan is reserved for highlights — streak dots, hover state, the spark glow. Never decorative.
  • Serif (Fraunces) for titles, sans (Plus Jakarta) for everything else — gives entries a published, considered feel.
